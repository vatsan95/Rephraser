## The launch post

> I spent six months nights-and-weekends building a free offline AI text rewriter. Today it's live on Mac and Windows.
>
> The tool is called Rephraser. Why I built it:
>
> Every AI writing tool I was using — Grammarly, Copilot, Wordtune — was shipping my text to a cloud server. For contract language, for medical notes, for personal messages, that wasn't a tradeoff I wanted.
>
> So Rephraser runs entirely on your laptop. The AI model (Gemma 3, Qwen, Phi-4 — you pick) is downloaded once and then everything happens on-device. Works with wifi off. Free forever. MIT licensed.
>
> The workflow is simple: select text anywhere, hit ⌥⇧R (Mac) or Ctrl+Alt+R (Windows), a small panel appears with a rewrite, Enter pastes it. Works in Word, Outlook, Slack, VS Code, Chrome — anything where Ctrl+C works.
>
> Three things I learned building it:
>
> 1. Native beats Electron for small tools. Mac is 8 MB, Windows is 15 MB. Grammarly's desktop is 250 MB.
>
> 2. On-device inference is fast enough for interactive UX now. 1.4 seconds median rephrase on an M2 Air. Even a 4-core Intel laptop runs a 0.5B model at real-time speed.
>
> 3. "Free + offline + open source" is a real wedge. Every mainstream competitor hits one of the three; none hit all three.
>
> If you write for a living — emails, Slack, docs — give it 30 seconds. Brutal feedback welcome.
>
